Protecting Personal Data online

    What is personal data?

  • It means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person
  • Personal data can identify someone's name, identification number, location data etc.

    Why is it important to protect your personal data online?

  • 1. Prevent identity theft
  • 2. Defend yourself in legal proceedings
  • 3. Protect your financial information

Having your personal data online is dangerous and it is important to know what are the ways to protect them so that your identity is safe

Here are 5 ways to protect your personal data online:

    1. Create secure passwords

  • Many people use simple and common passwords, such as passwords or 123456, which can be easily guessed by attackers. A strong password is usually long and contains a random mix of letters, numbers and special characters. This will make it difficult for someone to discover your passwords
  • You should create different and secure passwords for each account so that it will be more difficult for attackers to access your personal data

    2. Use a VPN

  • If you need to use public wifi, a virtual private network(VPN) service will secure your connection by encrypting all data that travels between your device and the internet.
  • Additionally, by replacing your real IP address with the one of the VPN server, a VPN improves your privacy by preventing ISPs and advertisers from tracking your physical location and building a detailed profile of your online interests, habits and behaviours

    3. Set up 2FA

  • Two factor authentication(2FA) increases the security of your accounts by requiring at least 2 forms of verification, such as your account password and a one time password(OTP). Even if someone knows your account password, they still can't get access without the second factor
  • Moreover, if you get a 2FA notification that you didn't request, it's a warning that someone is trying to access your account, so you can quickly change your password.

    4. Limit the information you share online

  • The less personal information you share on the internet, the fewer opportunities cyber-criminals have to steal your data which can be used for scams, doxing or social engineering attacks
  • When creating or updating online profiles, provide only compulsory information and skip the optional fields asking for details that could be used to track or impersonate you

    5. Be wary of phishing attacks

  • Always be careful with emails, messages and websites that ask for personal information sice it could be a scam or phising attacks
  • Never click on links or download attachments from senders you don't recognise or weren't expecting
