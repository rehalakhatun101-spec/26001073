Types of Cyber Attacks

Phishing
Tricking people into giving away their private information stored, usually by email.
Malware
Harmful software that messes with your computer or spies on you.
Ransomware
Locks your files and asks for money in order to unlock them.
DoS
Flooding a website or server to slow it down or crash it.
Man-in-the-Middle
Someone intercepting your data secretly over a network.
Phishing Malware Ransomware DoS Man-in-the-Middle

Phishing

If you received a suspicious email that seems off, that’s usually phishing. It's when attackers send fake messages trying to trick you into giving up your personal info.

  • Emails pretending to be from someone you know or trust
  • Links that lead to fake websites
  • Requests for passwords, credit card numbers, or other sensitive details

Malware

Malware is a bad software. Like viruses or spyware, it messes with your computer or steals your data without you knowing.

  • Viruses that can damage or delete your files
  • Spyware quietly spying on your activity
  • Trojans that look safe but are actually harmful

Ransomware

It locks up your files and demands money if you want them back.

  • Encrypts your important files so you can't open them
  • Shows ransom notes with instructions on how to pay
  • Often sneaks in through phishing emails

DoS Attack

Short for Denial of Service, this attack floods websites or servers with tons of traffic until they crash or slow to a crawl.

  • Bombards servers with more requests than they can handle
  • Makes websites go offline or unreachable
  • Can distract security teams from other attacks happening at the same time

Man-in-the-Middle Attack

A Man-in-the-Middle Attack means that someone is secretly listening in or even messing with your online conversations.

  • Steals passwords and sensitive info without you knowing
  • Changes messages between two people without either side realizing
  • Often happens on public Wi-Fi networks that aren’t secure
