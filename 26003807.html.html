Importance of Passwords

ꨄ︎ Prevent identity theft

  • weak passwords allow hackers to easily access personal account
  • once in, attackers can steal your identity, access sensitive info, or impersonate you online

ꨄ︎ Protect online accounts

  • your email, social media, and work accounts all rely on passwords
  • strong passwords help keep hackers out, preventing data loss or misuse

ꨄ︎ Block unauthorised access

  • hackers use automated tools to guess simple or reused passwords
  • a complex and unique password makes this so much more harder, reducing the risks of breaches

ꨄ︎ Reduce risks of cyber attacks

  • weak passwords are one of the top causes of data breaches
  • in 2023, many attacks were successful because people reused or chose easy passwords

Multi-factor authentication

ꨄ︎ What is multi-factor authentication?

    multi-factor authentication (MFA for short) is a security process that requires users to provide two or more verification factors to gain access to an account or system

    ꨄ︎ why is it so important?

    • adds and extra layer of security by requiring a second form of verification (eg, a code to your phone or fingerprints)
    • it helps protect sensitive information such as emails, bank accs, and even social media by making it harder for hackers to access your accounts although they have your password

      • ꨄ︎ what are the methods of MFA?

      • sms-based authentication - generates OTP sent via SMS to user's phone numbers
      • biometrics authentication - face ID, fingerprints, iris pattern etc
      • application-based authentication - uses authenticator to generate OTP

Whats the difference between MFA and passwords?

MFA Passwords
A single layer of security Multiple layers of security
You enter your password to log in You enter your password + an extra code or method
Easier to guess, steal or leak Harder to hack because it needs something to leak -- like phone number or fingerprint
