 5 Tips to Protect Yourself from Online Scams

5 TIPS to Protect Yourself from Online Scams before you close this website ;)

These are more or less the effective methods recommended by people with experience and after some research

#1

Watch Out for Pressure Tactics

Scammers may try to rush or scare you into doing what they want. Take your time and don’t be pushed into decisions.

#2

Verify Before Taking Action

Go straight to a company’s official website to verify contact details before responding to suspicious messages.

#3

Don’t Click on Suspicious Links

Avoid clicking links in emails or messages from unknown or unusual sources—even if they look legitimate.

#4

Use Trusted Payment Methods

Only pay through secure and official channels. Avoid third-party websites or unfamiliar apps for financial transactions.

#5

Report Scams

Whether you were affected or not, reporting helps stop scammers and protects others from becoming victims.

Watch out for pressure tactics Scammers often create a false sense of urgency to pressure you into making quick decisions, such as claiming your account will be locked or that you owe money and must pay immediately. Take a moment to pause and think. If you receive an unexpected message or call urging immediate action, verify its authenticity by contacting the organization directly using official contact information.
Verify Before Taking Action Before responding to unsolicited messages or requests, always confirm their legitimacy. Go directly to the official website of the company or organization in question. Use contact details from their official site to verify the message's authenticity. Avoid using contact information provided in the suspicious message itself.
Don’t Click on Suspicious Links Scammers often include malicious links in emails, text messages, or social media posts to trick you into revealing personal information or downloading harmful software. Hover over links to preview the URL before clicking. If the link looks suspicious or unfamiliar, do not click on it. Instead, visit the official website directly by typing the address into your browser.
Use Trusted Payment Methods Scammers may ask you to pay using unconventional methods like gift cards, wire transfers, or cryptocurrency. Use secure and traceable payment methods, such as credit cards or trusted payment platforms. Be cautious of requests for payment through unusual channels.
Report Scams Reporting scams helps authorities track and combat fraudulent activities, potentially preventing others from falling victim. If you encounter a scam, report it to relevant authorities or organizations. In Singapore, you can report scams to the Anti-Scam Centre or the Consumer Protection (Fair Trading) Division.
